A Chakra a Day

Come what may


The comprehensive systemized structure of the chakra system can help you regardless of if chakras truly exist or not.

You don't have to be a Christian to follow some of the 10 commandments. Similarly, the chakras don't require you to believe in them for you to find usefulness in their philosophy and teachings.



I was skeptical when I first stumbled upon the chakras, but I quickly realized how thoughtful and intelligent their structure is, and how helpful they could be to the average person who would overlook this as "hippie bullshit", and I get it. I'm not sure I buy into homeopathic stuff either, but it is a shame that the chakra system has been lumped into

"new age nonsense", versus "self-help and development".

If you want to practically improve any of the following areas in your life without all of the "woo woo", you've happened upon a resource that will detail methods to do just that (but yea, the hippies and yogis of the world did discover this first):

  • Feeling grounded, safe, supported, and energized
  • Control over your emotions, desires, and capacity for pleasure
  • Willpower, sense of purpose, and strength
  • Unconditional love and compassion for yourself and others
  • Effective, tactful, and confident communication abilities
  • Trust, intuition, and acceptance

The chakra system integrates ALL aspects of life by categorizing our experiences, emotions, and physicality into 7 different areas that balance and effect one another. They have a Westernized name and their original name:

  1. the ROOT chakra (Muladhara)
  2. the SACRAL chakra (Svadhishthana)
  3. the SOLAR PLEXUS chakra (Manipura)
  4. the HEART chakra (Anahata)
  5. the THROAT chakra (Vishuddha)
  6. the THIRD EYE chakra (Ajna)
  7. the CROWN chakra (Sahasrara)
